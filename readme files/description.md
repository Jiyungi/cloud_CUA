. Tracks & Challenges
You must choose one track:

Track 1: Computer Use

Build agents that see, click, and type across real desktop applications. Ship something ambitious, creative, and useful.

YOU MUST USE H COMPANY COMPUTER USE AGENTS.

Track 2: Browser Use

Build agents that navigate the messy real web: logins, popups, cookie walls, infinite scroll.

YOU MUST USE H COMPANY COMPUTER USE AGENTS.

Track 3: Free-For-All

Any idea using H Company Agents !

Side challenges (you can in 0 to all challenges):

Nvidia Challenge: Run the H Company models through NemoClaw. The best integration and the best overall project win.
Voice Challenge: best use of Gradium. Integrate voice into your agent, TTS or STT or both. The most fluid or most creative interaction wins.
2. Judging Criteria
100 points total, five equal criteria of 20 each:

Technicality (20)
Creativity (20)
Usefulness (20)
Demo (20)
Track / sponsor alignment (20)
3. Presentation format
You'll need to submit:

a 2-minutes demo of your project
the link to your github repo
a short description of your project
The judging will take place in 2 rounds.

1st round:

5 minutes per team:
Pitch (1:30): the problem, your approach plus high-level scheme
Demo (1:30) of the product, live.
Q&A (2:00)
2nd round:

between 8 and 10 finalists will be selected to pitch on stage in front of everyone
finalists will be announced one by one and should be ready to pitch and present their project when called
3 minutes per team:
Pitch (1:30): the problem, your approach plus high-level scheme
Demo (1:30)
no questions
4. Submission rules
You must use H Company models for your project.
Build entirely during the event. No prior commits to the repo.
Teams of 2 to 5.
Open-source libs, APIs, and pre-trained models allowed if properly credited.
Submit all code and demos before the deadline to be eligible.
Follow the law, ethical AI practices, and the Code of Conduct. Never use data without consent.
Use partner and sponsor APIs per their terms of service.
Organizers may disqualify any team for rule violations or unfair play.
5. Code of Conduct
Treat everyone with courtesy, whatever their background or experience.
Zero tolerance for demeaning, threatening, or discriminatory behavior.
Compete fairly: help, share, don't sabotage.
Respect the venue and equipment; clean up after yourself.
Wellbeing over winning: rest, eat, check on teammates.
If something feels wrong, tell an organizer. Reports stay confidential.
